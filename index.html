Understanding Mutual Funds & SIPs

Simple explanations to help you invest confidently and build long-term wealth.

SIP is a disciplined way of investing a fixed amount every month in mutual funds. It helps you invest regularly without worrying about market ups and downs.

  • Small monthly investments (like a monthly saving habit)
  • Reduces risk through rupee cost averaging
  • Best for salaried & business individuals
  • Ideal for long-term goals like retirement & children’s education
Types of SIP:
  • Regular SIP: Fixed amount every month
  • Step-Up SIP: Increases every year
  • Flexible SIP: As per cash flow
  • Perpetual SIP: No end date

SWP allows you to withdraw a fixed amount from your mutual fund investments at regular intervals. It provides liquidity while keeping your money invested.

  • Regular monthly or quarterly withdrawals
  • Helps generate regular income from investments
  • Ideal for retired individuals or for specific goals
  • Reduces the need to redeem the entire investment at once

Lumpsum investment is a one-time investment in a mutual fund scheme. Best for investors with available funds to invest at once.

  • Invest a large amount at a single time
  • Potential for higher returns if timed well
  • Suitable for experienced investors or tax savings
  • Less frequent monitoring compared to SIP

Types of Funds

Equity funds invest in shares of companies to grow your money over the long term. They may fluctuate in the short term but give higher returns over time.

  • Large Cap Funds: Stable companies, lower risk
  • Mid Cap Funds: Growing companies, moderate risk
  • Small Cap Funds: High growth potential, higher risk
  • Flexi Cap Funds: Invest across all company sizes

Hybrid funds invest in both equity and debt to balance growth and safety. Suitable for investors who want moderate risk.

  • Balanced Advantage Funds
  • Aggressive Hybrid Funds
  • Conservative Hybrid Funds

Debt funds invest in fixed-income instruments like bonds. They offer stability and are suitable for short-term goals.

  • Liquid Funds
  • Short Duration Funds
  • Corporate Bond Funds

ELSS funds help you save tax under Section 80C while investing in equity markets.

  • Tax benefit up to ₹1.5 lakh
  • 3-year lock-in (shortest among tax-saving options)
  • Good returns over long term
